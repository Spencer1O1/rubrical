Module 6: Final Creative Project & Reflection

Module 6: Final Creative Project & Reflection
Due: Fri Aug 7, 2026 11:59pmDue: Fri Aug 7, 2026 11:59pm8/7/2026
Ungraded, 50 Possible Points50 Points Possible
In Progress
NEXT UP: Submit Assignment
Unlimited Attempts Allowed

For your final project, you’ll explore and apply what you’ve learned in Arts Symposium through a personalized creative endeavor. Choose one of the following options to explore your ideas and creative process. Each project must include:

  • Your creative work, submitted via Box, SharePoint, YouTube, or another accessible link

  • A reflection of at least 300 words on your creative process and the meaning behind your work (include word count)

Project Options:

Visual Artwork or Photography
Submit an original drawing, painting, digital artwork, or photograph that reflects a unique idea or theme. Include both your project link and 300-word reflection.

Music Composition or Performance
Submit an original musical composition or performance expressing a creative idea or emotional response. Include both your project link and 300-word reflection.

Dance, Movement, or Short Film Media Project
Record and share a choreographed or improvised dance, gesture piece, movement-based performance, or short film reflecting an original idea or theme. Include both your project link and 300-word reflection.

Theater or Monologue Performance
Perform and record a monologue, dramatic reading, or original theatrical piece that communicates a personal or artistic idea. Include both your project link and 300-word reflection.

Creative Writing
Submit an original poem, short story, or narrative exploring an artistic idea or theme. Include both your project link and 300-word reflection.

Equivalent Creative Project
Propose an alternative format—such as an installation, interactive media slideshow, short video, podcast, research-based essay, or interdisciplinary work—that aligns with the goals of this project. Email your proposal in advance to Dr. Olson at kevin.olson@usu.edu for approval, and submit both the project link and 300-word reflection.
